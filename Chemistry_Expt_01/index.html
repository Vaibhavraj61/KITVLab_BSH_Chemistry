Experiment No. 1: Standardization of Solutions

Aim : To prepare 0.1 molar oxalic acid solution and standardization of NaOH solution.

A) Preparation of 0.1 M oxalic acid:

Step No 1:

Weigh out accurately about 3.15 g of oxalic acid dihydrate crystals (H2C2O4. 2H2O) on analytical balance.

Step No 2:

Transfer the weighed oxalic acid dihydrate crystals into a standard volumetric flask (250 mL) and dissolve in minimum amount of distilled water.

Step No 3:

Make the volume of solution up to the mark in the standard flask to obtain 0.1 M oxalic acid. Shake it by tilting the flask upside down.

B) Standardization of NaOH solution:

Step No 1:

Fill a burette with the standard oxalic acid solution.

Step No 2:

By pipette take 25.0 ml of the 0.1 M alkali solution, run it into a conical flask, add two or three drops of phenolphthalein solution.

Step No 3:

Make a note of the burette reading and run in the acid from the burette until the solution becomes colorless.

Step No 4:

4) Now repeat the titration with further portions of 25.0 ml of the alkali solution until two readings are obtained which agree to 0.10 ml.

Step No 5:

5) Calculate the concentration of the alkali.

Calculation:

Formula:

N1 x V1 = N2 x V2

N1= Normality of oxalic acid = 0.1 M

V1= Volume of oxalic acid used =12.0 ml

N2= Normality of NaOH solution = ___M

V2= Volume of alkali taken = 25 ml

V1= Volume of oxalic acid used =12.0 ml

RESULT:
Concentration of NaOH solution = 0.1 mol/lit.
